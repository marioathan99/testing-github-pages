Chapter 1: Introduction to Functional Microanatomy of Neurons

Overview

In this chapter, we explore the structural and functional organization of neurons—the basic building blocks of the nervous system.

Key Concepts

  • Neurons: Specialized cells that transmit information by electrical and chemical signaling.
  • Main Structures:
    • Dendrites: Receive incoming signals.
    • Soma (cell body): Contains the nucleus and integrates signals.
    • Axon: Conducts electrical impulses away from the cell body.
    • Axon terminals: Communicate with other neurons or target tissues.
  • Synapses: Points of communication between neurons.
  • Support cells (glia): Provide structure, protection, and metabolic support for neurons.

Summary

The microanatomy of neurons underpins all of brain function. By understanding their structure and roles, we set the stage for deeper study into how the brain processes information.
