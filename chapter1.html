Chapter 1: Introduction to Functional Microanatomy of Neurons

Overview

In this chapter, we explore the structural and functional organization of neurons‚Äîthe foundational units of the nervous system.

Key Concepts

  • Neurons: Specialized cells that transmit information by electrical and chemical signaling.
  • Main Structures:
    • Dendrites: Receive incoming signals.
    • Soma (cell body): Contains the nucleus, integrates signals.
    • Axon: Conducts impulses away from the soma.
    • Axon terminals: Communicate with other cells.
  • Synapses: Points where neurons communicate.
  • Glial cells: Provide structure & support for neurons.

Summary

Neuronal microanatomy provides the structural foundation for complex brain functions. From signal receiving (dendrites) to output (axons), each part has a critical role. Understanding these is the first step to decoding the brain.
