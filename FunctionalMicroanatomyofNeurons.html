üî¥ Cell Body (Soma)

Contains the nucleus and most organelles. This is where most cellular metabolism occurs and where the cell integrates incoming signals from dendrites.

üåø Dendrites

Branch-like extensions that receive signals from other neurons. They act like antennae, collecting information from multiple sources.

üîµ Axon

Long projection that carries electrical signals away from the cell body. Can be very short or over a meter long in humans!

üå± Axon Terminals

End points of the axon that release neurotransmitters to communicate with other neurons, muscles, or glands.

üü° Myelin Sheath

Fatty insulation around the axon that speeds up signal transmission. Like insulation on electrical wires!

üß† Gray Matter

Contains:

  • Cell bodies (soma)
  • Dendrites
  • Unmyelinated axons
  • Synapses

Function: Information processing and computation

Location: Cerebral cortex, brain nuclei

ü§ç White Matter

Contains:

  • Myelinated axons
  • Oligodendrocytes
  • Few cell bodies

Function: Information transmission between brain regions

Appearance: Lighter due to fatty myelin sheaths
